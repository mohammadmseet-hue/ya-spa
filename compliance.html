Founder & legal action plan

Get the licensing spine right — before a single riyal goes to product.

At-home beauty is legal in Saudi Arabia, but it is establishment-anchored: the right to send a beautician to a customer's home flows from a licensed business, not from an individual freelancer. The single most common way founders fail in this category is assuming an "Uber-for-beauty" freelancer pool is allowed. It is not. This checklist sequences the real path. Tick items as you clear them — progress saves in your browser.

Go / no-go, not a tweak. A freelance work document (وثيقة العمل الحر) legitimizes the person — it does not authorize at-home delivery. Home service requires a Balady "Providing Home Services Permit" tied to a valid commercial registration + municipal women's-salon license. Confirm this pathway with a KSA lawyer first.

Entity & registration

Stand up the legal vehicle and the consumer-facing registrations.

Incorporate & obtain a Commercial Registration (CR)

Form the operating company and obtain a CR whose business activity covers beauty/salon services. Choose an entity with liability protection (an LLC), not a sole proprietorship — it carries no liability shield for in-home incidents.

Ministry of Commerce

Foreign-ownership license (if applicable)

If any founder/investor is non-Saudi, obtain a MISA investment license before the CR. Confirm the activity is open to foreign investment.

MISA

Register the store on Maroof

Mandatory for e-commerce. Required for payment-gateway onboarding and consumer trust; non-registration carries fines.

Ministry of Commerce · Maroof

File the trademark — Arabic & Latin

Register "يا سبا" and "Ya Spa" plus the figurative mark with SAIP, in Class 44 (beauty services) and Class 35 (marketplace). Expect "Spa" to be disclaimed; the Arabic figurative wordmark is the protectable asset. Clear against Glamera, Ruwaa, Bookr, Yammak first.

SAIP

Secure domains & handles

Register yaspa.sa + the Arabic IDN ياسبا.السعودية via SaudiNIC, plus yaspa.com. Lock @yaspa on Snapchat first, then Instagram/TikTok.

SaudiNIC

The supply-licensing spine

The hard, defensible core. This is what makes at-home delivery lawful.

Obtain a municipal women's-salon (beautification) license

Secure a Balady/Baladiya commercial license for women's beautification (التزيين النسائي) whose activity code is eligible for a home-services add-on. This is the base the home permit attaches to.

MoMRAH · Balady

Obtain the "Providing Home Services Permit"

The keystone. Issued electronically via the Balady platform, anchored to the valid commercial license, and added per worker (a worker can hold only one active permit). It auto-revokes if the parent license lapses.

MoMRAH · Balady

Appoint a Saudi female salon manager

Mandated for women's beauty establishments. Build the org chart around this from day one.

MoMRAH / HRSD

Plan Saudization (Nitaqat) from the start

Beauty salons face Saudization targets (~50% Saudi staff) and a cap on foreign workers. Model your hiring mix to stay in the green band; tap Productive Families / Monsha'at female-talent channels.

HRSD

Collect Health + Professional certificates per provider

Each provider needs a valid Health Certificate and a Professional Certificate for women's beauty services — required documents for the home-services permit.

Municipality / Health

Run security background checks & ID verification

Verify identity and run a background check on every provider before listing — and surface the result as a visible trust badge (the product differentiator).

Internal / vetting vendor

Source SFDA-compliant consumables (sealed kits)

Providers use platform-supplied, SFDA-approved, single-use sealed kits. Keep product OFF the consumer retail surface to avoid the SFDA importer/distributor regime until scale justifies it.

SFDA

Data & consumer protection

You process sensitive data (home address + skin/health + payment). Enforcement is live.

Build PDPL compliance in from day one

Explicit, plain-language consent at the point address/health data is collected; lawful-basis mapping; data-subject rights flows. Fines reach SAR 5M and SDAIA is actively enforcing.

SDAIA · PDPL

Appoint a DPO & register on the National Data Governance Platform

Given sensitive-data processing at scale, appoint a Data Protection Officer and complete required registrations.

SDAIA

Host in-Kingdom & control cross-border transfers

Use a KSA-region cloud; apply SDAIA's data-transfer controls/standard clauses for anything leaving the Kingdom.

SDAIA

Comply with the E-Commerce Law

All-in transparent pricing (service + transport + VAT), clear return/cancellation policy, published privacy policy. Address marketplace-operator liability for provider conduct in your terms (get implementing-regulation legal review).

Ministry of Commerce

Publish a transparent cancellation & lateness policy

Tiered, fair, and visible at checkout — and the engine for your "on-time-or-credited" promise.

Risk & insurance

In-home injury, property damage and theft exposure are real — turn cover into a trust pitch.

Carry public-liability & professional-indemnity insurance

No statute mandates it for this model, but in-home exposure makes it essential. Bond providers and codify coverage in your terms.

Insurer

Draft platform Terms & Conditions

Allocate provider-conduct liability, define service standards, the guarantee, dispute resolution, and the customer/provider relationship.

Legal counsel

Build an incident-response SLA & female safety line

A credible, fast process for safety/theft incidents — the anti-incumbent promise. Operationally and contractually defined.

Payments

Integrate licensed providers — do not build regulated financial products.

Integrate Mada, Apple Pay, STC Pay & cards

Onboard a licensed payment gateway; charge upfront at booking (market norm) to eliminate no-show and billing-dispute risk.

Payment provider

Plug in BNPL via Tabby / Tamara

Both are SAMA-licensed. Integrate them — do NOT build your own instalment/credit product (that needs a SAMA finance licence). Decisive on bridal tickets.

Tabby / Tamara · SAMA-licensed

Five traps that kill this business

Freelancer-only "Uber for beauty."

A وثيقة العمل الحر alone does not authorize at-home delivery. The Balady home-services permit is establishment-anchored. This is the #1 founder-killer.

Sponsored domestic workers moonlighting.

Using domestic-worker visa holders as beauticians is tasattur/visa abuse — fines reach SAR 20,000 per violation. Audit sponsorship status at onboarding.

Any mixed-gender flow.

Female-only providers for female clients is a hard rule, enforced via entry-restriction, a Saudi female manager, and Saudization. Keep it absolute.

Medical-aesthetic scope creep.

Injectables, lasers and clinical procedures are MoH-licensed-facility territory. Stay in low-medical-risk beauty; gate massage to phase 2 with legal clearance.

Retailing cosmetics in-app.

Selling physical product triggers the full SFDA importer/distributor regime (eCosma, GSO 1943 labelling, recall & adverse-event reporting). Keep to sealed platform kits until scale.
